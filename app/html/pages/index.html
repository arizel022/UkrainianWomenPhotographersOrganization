About

Ukrainian Women Photography Organization is a comunity dedicated to women photographers. Our goal is unite women photographers around the world and aspecially in Ukraine. We want to inspre women and helping them discharge their talents more fully in a world that is becoming increasingly competitive and precarious. Increasing awareness of the Ukrainian photography around the world and raising the level of Ukrainian photography with educational projects.

Hanna Melnykova

president and curator

As a teacher Anna developed her own study program in the field of visual arts and taught at the Bird in Fligh school (Kiyv), Pinchuck art center (Kiyv), Karazin National University (Kharkiv), Kharkiv Academy of Design and Arts. In 2020, during the lockdown, she created the Ukrainian Women Photographers Organization. Curator of international exhibitions and residencies in America, Switzerland, Germany and the Netherlands. As an artist she is currently researching and working with themes of psychology antropology and society through experimental techniques in photography. Her photographs are in private collections in the Netherlands, Italy, Belgium. Her first book “Largo” dedicated to the study of gender in modern society was represented during the UNSEEN festival in Amsterdam.

Yana Hryhorenko

curator of the NFT collection

Member of the Ukrainian women photography organization, photographer, artist, curator of nft projects. She devoted most of her life (2004-2018) to a career in the field of marketing, from an advertising copywriter to an art director. In 2019, she began studying photography and art, as well as working on the first documentary series "CHILDREACH" (the architecture of children's playgrounds in modern Ukraine). Currently works in the field of conceptual photography and explores the portrait genre. In nft from 2021.

Education

For the Ukrainian women photography organization, education is a key aspect of the work in addition to exhibitions and publications. Our educational activities take place on residencies at conferences and as guests of other institutions and also online. The rapid changes in photography call for active engagement. We are faced with new kinds of images every day. Photographers no longer necessarily press the shutter release, but copy, edit, remix and recontextualise existing material. They write codes and let algorithms generate images. We are very open in our engagement with these changes, researching contemporary developments and participating in the discourse on photography. We are committed to a reflective examination of photographic medium and wish to encourage an independent, thoughtful and creative use of photography. We welcome those who are just starting their creative journey, and those who are already professionally engaged in photography.
